Welcome to PriMSA

Take Me to PriMSA


Through the use of Multiple Sequence Alignment (MSA) of homologous mRNA sequences for the gene of interest using ClustalW, PriMSA helps design primers for Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR). The user can either choose to query the NCBI database for the sequences or input their own in FASTA format.

There are five different categories of output for PriMSA. A brief description for each output is included below.

Consensus Sequence

Pictorial Representation of the alignment of one sets of primers with the consensus sequence from Primer3

Phylogenetic Tree

A Newick tree of the protein sequences aligned

Frequency Graph

A bar diagram showing total number (frequency) in a triplet codon where conservation is less than 50%

Table of Results

Contains the results from Primer3 in a tabular format.

Multiple Sequence Alignment Viewer

Integrates MSAViewer to show the alignment of protein sequences translated from the mRNA sequences

Graphs

Graphical Representation showing the distribution of nucleotide bases in the the alignment.
